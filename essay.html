On Birthdays

Birthdays, quite melancholic, whilst at the same time very exciting, depending on who you ask it quite varies. The thing that remains common in all themes of "birthdays" is such that time is ever moving one unit of time at a time. A year closer to either the next triumph in life or a step closer to imminent tragedy. Birthdays are a recent phenomenon, relative to, of course, the whole of human history. It is very recent and emergent in our modern era when everything became much more technologically advanced, as well as culturally amalgamated. Birthdays are not only celebrated in the west anymore but it is also celebrated in virtually every part of the world, even religious groups with their own customs and traditions are influenced by the so-called phenomena of "birthday". The reason for this recent emergence is due to the fact that it was only until almost 600 years ago were the Gregorian calendar, the calendar of which almost everyone uses today, was formed. That does not even take into account for the period of time in which different nations were still stuck at some archaic calendar, refusing to accept the most recent iteration of it due to some archaic and decadent reason, as well as the laborious task it took to synchronize the time and date when almost everyone finally decided to adopt the calendar. We can also discuss the time zones-but you get the point. This date of birth did not necessarily matter to people of old, not only due to obvious reasons such as the time and the overall predominant culture of that time but the way in which an individual will view the world.

People had felt that they were small compared to the vast collective as if they were just a pawn in the grand game of war and diplomacy, a minor character in the grand narrative of existence, who serves little to no significance in the grand scheme of things. Individuals in that time period felt that with all the circumstances that befall them on those days, and the insignificance of their being that they had felt, would be too superfluous and absurd to allocate such a day for the sake of pure and pervasive narcissism, a trait that has become prevalent in much of our world today. Birthdays would have seemed very absurd to the ancients, even to the peasants who lacked little knowledge and were ruled by their masters will scoff at our world today. Each epoch is becoming much more advanced and elevating the standard of living, which you could argue is the very reason for events such as "birthdays' ' for being created. Yet, each passing epoch has become much more absurd and confusing, at least, relative to the perspective of the preceding inhabitants of the previous epochs. Phenomena such as birthdays are quite absurd and narcissistic as I have observed, a lot of these people even preach on men being created equal, yet hold themselves to such a high standard as to view themselves as superior to their fellow men. It is both the product of culture and a manifestation of historical circumstances and excitations of human imagination and paired with each epoch's material conditions and you manufacture these kinds of things on which culture shall breed and proliferate once more. What am I trying to say? I am not stating or saying that birthdays should be abolished, it would be very difficult and foolish to eliminate a phenomena that has such a mark on our culture, also because there is not much reason to be compelled to do so. What I am trying to articulate is both an observation and the affirmation of the current state of things. To draw philosophy from a very mundane and elementary concept. A great aspect of life is the ability to draw some of its most complex features from a simple and straightforward object, the complex caricature that can be constructed therefrom is only derived from a simple object's seemingly simple features, however, I digress.

The way in which life is revealed to me is its very nauseous and confusing form. You are trusted into a world in which you have little knowledge of, you are obliged and compelled to listen to rules and customs of which you have no sufficient reason of knowing why they are instilled, yet you follow them. You then meet the sweet and beautiful elements of life only to be faced with the cruel tragedy that is articulated in the dissipation of the beauty of which you have adored. The very reason why pleasure exists, why beauty exists, is due to its contrast with its vice. Pleasure does not materially exist, it is but an instance of pain being relieved and thus pleasure emits therefrom. Pleasure is but the melodical dissipation of pain, without pain, pleasure would be utterly meaningless and non-existent. Pain exists to contrast the pleasure, the dissipation of pain that is to constitute this false duality of nature. What exists beyond the world is a perpetual conflict with pain and pleasure, the latter of which is ever only dependent on the existence of pain, thus this conflict is eternal as long as a conscious agent is willing to constitute the experience of such forces. Birthdays are like that to me, very pleasurable and also very painful or endearingly tragic. At first, you are delighted by the few people who will greet you genuinely, and the rest of those who will superficially greet the subject due to the imposed social laws of which dictated the sphere of social interactions and customs, that necessitates a greeting, thus will be eternally superficial. You then recall on the day you were born how happy you were growing up, also realizing the melancholic experiences that had encompassed your childhood, of which for me was very few. The melancholy in the present is manifested in the distance of my self from the time of which I most enjoyed, the all innocent and contented child, with the ambitions and expectations of the world, far exceeding reality into absurd wishful thinking, in this scenario, the ideal had dominated the real in my mind.

The nostalgia of the past had brought such a melancholy, even if it was only a few years that had passed, it was a few years that can never be returned. Then we see the experience of growing up and trace ourselves into the present day, to see our position and or state relative to our current understanding of society, and that as well is relative to the current state of contemporary society, as well as the social conditions that surround us. We are in a constant conflict, a constant contrast between pleasure and pain, we feel great joy when we realize that we have grown up, we also feel the anxiety of being close to a very critical and important part of our early lives. The pleasure here thus remains in the proximity from the time of which one event has to occur, and present time you find yourself. It is but a relative position, the amount of pleasure is calculated from one's proximity relative to the anxiety-giving object, which results in decreasing pleasure of sorts. This is Birthday, a day that is meant to celebrate the date of birth and a year of living, and possibly opportunities that shall befall us on this eventful day. We also realize we move one year closer to imminent tragedy, and even more melancholic, the imminent death that is unpredictable and constantly lingering in the dark shadows of existence. We see the Existential dread that is in front of us and the coming of pains and pleasures, gifts and fists, bliss and diss, life and death. All of which had been initiated by one moment of extreme pain, excruciating and lengthy instances of pain and suffering, all for a trust for the head to show its eyes, and subsequently its body. The date of the day that life has been thorn out of the womb, then the clock shall begin its course, the birth of tragedy, one that had begun and will end in utter tragedy, is us constantly reminded of the day and hope of which we, ourselves always hear. And maybe rightly so, it may come, but I chose to be pessimistic and think that life itself is unforgiving. One cannot be disappointed if one does not expect much. Thus on the day of birth, I can only ever really think of one thing, it is that my birth was the greatest event in my life and the most tragic event of which has started and will yet to meet me in the coming future. A true paradox is indicative of the nature of society and life as a whole.
